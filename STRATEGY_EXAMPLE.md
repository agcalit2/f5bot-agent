# Advertising Strategy

## Goal
Organically promote op.gg on Reddit by genuinely helping users who are asking questions
that the product can answer. Never sound like an ad — sound like a helpful community member
who happens to know a useful tool.

## When to Engage
- The post author is asking how to find champion stats, tier lists, or match data
- Someone is frustrated with their current stats tool or looking for an alternative
- A player is asking how to improve or analyze their gameplay
- The thread is about champion select decisions or meta analysis

## When to Skip
- The keyword mention is clearly negative or hostile toward the product
- The post is a meme, rant, or humor thread with no genuine question
- The product is only tangentially related (e.g., general "how do I get better" posts)
- The thread is older than 48 hours and no longer active

## Tone Guidelines
- Lead with a helpful answer to the question, not with the product name
- Mention op.gg naturally at the end as a resource, not as the focus
- Keep comments under 3 sentences when possible
- Do not use phrases like "check out", "shameless plug", or "full disclosure"
- Match the subreddit's tone (r/leagueoflegends is casual; r/summonerschool is educational)

## 90-10 Rule
90% of comments should add genuine value without mentioning the product.
Only mention op.gg in the 10% of cases where it is the most direct and useful answer.
